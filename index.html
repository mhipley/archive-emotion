Notes on an archive

Look back to a past moment and the view widens as we keep growing distant. A new topology forms.
Sometimes this shape gifts deeper understanding, but sometimes we see something that was never real.
There is nothing solid in sand.
Maybe archives can help us remember, and shelter our memories from the distorting flow of time.
Archives transfigure ephemera and trace an outline around the fragments within to give them familiar form.
This is how the archive becomes a stable location. A firm foundation - from which we can take flight.
Because archiving is time travel.
By holding our memories in time, the archive is a place we can return to - even though there's no way back.
As the past prints the future into the present, this portal also goes forward.
The archive is an object through which a message can be sent.
Within a future, and in all the din, one more beacon.
He was here, and I loved him.
