Notes on an archive

When we return to a familiar moment in the past, the view widens as we grow ever more distant. New topologies form.
Sometimes this shape gifts us deeper understanding, but sometimes we see something that was never real.
There is nothing solid in sand.
Maybe archiving is a way to remember that gives safe haven from the distorting flow of time.
Archives shelter ephemera, and trace a familiar outline around the fragments within to give them form. Archives find a way to make sense of the past.
This is how the archive becomes a stable location, to which we can look and reorient ourselves.
It becomes a firm foundation from which we can take flight, because archiving is time travel.
When our drifting memories are transfigured into a fixed record, the archive becomes a place we can return to - even though we know there's no way back.
As the past prints the future into the present, this portal also goes forward. The archive is an object through which a message can be sent.
Within a future, in all the din, there will be one more beacon.
He was here, and I loved him.
